## 📁 **Why organize workspace assets?**

Modern organizations create **fusion teams** — cross-functional groups using data and technology to deliver business outcomes faster. Those fusion teams share the same Workato workspace, so how you organize assets matters.

> 📌 Four reasons to organize:
> 
> - **🏢 The workspace is where business AND technology teams work together** to automate business processes.
> - **🔁 Collaborators often share application connections** or standard recipe functions across projects and teams.
> - **🚧 Organization establishes clear boundaries** for easier maintainability and proper asset governance.
> - **🔒 Separate working spaces** let teams manage their work while securing access to critical business data.

---

## 🗂️ **How to organize your workspace**

Three-level approach:

1. **🏢 Create functional area projects** — group automations based on **how your teams are organized** and how you want to separate access to data and recipes. Top-level projects (Finance, Sales, HR, IT, etc.).
2. **📂 Create folders** inside projects — group related recipes or connections. **Folders are for organization, not for security** — permissions do not apply here.
3. **🔐 Apply permissions at the functional area (project) level** — create roles and grant them access to specific projects.

> ⚠️ **Folders ≠ security boundary.** Two people with access to the same functional area project can see everything in every folder within it. Use functional area projects, not folders, for permission-boundary decisions.

---

## 🤝 **Best practices for sharing assets**

Two scenarios: sharing **within** a functional area and sharing **across** teams.

---

### 🏘️ Sharing assets within a functional area

For connections and standard recipe functions shared inside a single functional area:

- **📂 Use sub-folders** within the functional area project.
- **🔓 No extra permissions needed** — team members already have access to assets in projects they can see.
- **🔍 Improves discoverability and reuse** of standard functions and promotes standardization within the team.

> 📌 A team member can only see and use connections/recipe functions **in projects they have access to**. That's the discovery boundary — no extra permission requests required for anything already inside their project.

---

### 🌐 Sharing assets across teams

For connections and recipe functions used **workspace-wide** (across multiple functional areas):

- **🏛️ Create a separate top-level project** named `commons` or `shared assets`.
- **♻️ Manage the lifecycle of global assets independently** from the functional area projects.
- **🎫 Team members need access to BOTH** the shared assets project AND their functional area project.

> 📌 The **top-level shared project pattern** is the standard for global assets — application connections, callable recipes, connections to enterprise auth systems, etc. Cross-team assets don't belong inside any single team's project.

---

## 🗣️ **Use common vocabulary**

Beyond structure, **shared language** matters:

- Use a **common language** when communicating with team members and in change control processes.
- **Promote consistency and avoid duplication** of effort across team members.
- Refer to the **Workato Naming Conventions Best Practices** (also covered in the Technical Developer course, chapter 8.1) for details.

> 💡 The Recipe Lifecycle Management naming pattern from Technical Developer 8.1 works well here too: prefix + business-process ID + recipe number, e.g. `HRD 123-01 Create G-Suite Account`.

---

### 🧠 Quick recall

- What's the top-level organizational unit in a Workato workspace? (Functional area projects)
- What's the difference between folders and projects, permission-wise? (Projects are the permission boundary; folders are for organization only — no security controls.)
- Where should shared assets live? (In a separate top-level project — commonly named `commons` or `shared assets`.)
- How many projects does a team member need access to if they use both team-specific and shared assets? (`_____`) (Two — their functional area project AND the shared assets project.)
- What makes assets discoverable to a team member without extra permission requests? (Being in a project the team member already has access to.)
- Naming best practices are covered in what other Workato course? (Technical Developer chapter 8.1 — Recipe Lifecycle Management)

---

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Three-level org**: functional area projects (permissions) → folders (organization only) → assets. Never rely on folders for security.
- **Within-team sharing**: sub-folders inside the functional area project. Discoverability is automatic for anyone with project access.
- **Cross-team sharing**: a dedicated top-level `shared assets` (or `commons`) project. Members need access to both their functional area AND the shared project.
- **Common vocabulary** — naming conventions and terminology are governance too. Reuse the RLCM naming patterns from Technical Developer 8.1.
