## 🔑 **Managing user access**

Four ongoing practices for keeping user access clean and secure:

- **🔄 Consistently review user access changes** to validate: appropriate roles by environment, access to different projects, and proper authentication strategy.
- **🎫 Standardize and secure platform access** using your org-wide **Single Sign-On (SSO)** identity provider with Workato.
- **📱 Enforce two-factor authentication (2FA)** if you don't use SSO.
- **⏱️ Adjust the automatic session logout period** to align with your org-wide security policy.

> 📌 **SSO is the preferred authentication method** — it centralizes identity, respects corporate policies, and simplifies user lifecycle management. 2FA is the fallback when SSO isn't in play.

---

## 🎫 **SSO for a Workato team**

> 📌 Workato supports **SAML-based Single Sign-On (SSO)** for authorized access to multiple team members.

The important nuance about **enforced** SAML SSO:

> ⚠️ When you **enforce SAML SSO**, all team members **must authenticate through your identity provider**. They **cannot** access the team or its resources by logging in with a Workato username and password.

This is a hard cutover: enforcement means Workato username/password is fully disabled for the team. Plan accordingly — verify all users can authenticate through your IdP before flipping the switch.

---

## 🚀 **Establish a provisioning workflow**

Four practices for user onboarding:

- **📋 Establish a user onboarding process** to streamline the workflow and eliminate manual effort/errors for every user account provisioning.
- **🤖 Automate user onboarding** and create user accounts with appropriate role assignments using **SSO/Just-in-Time (SSO/JIT) provisioning**.
- **🏦 Centralize the access provisioning process** with appropriate reviews and approvals for non-standard requests.

> 📌 **SSO/JIT provisioning** is the ideal onboarding flow: a new hire logs in with their org credentials, and Workato **automatically creates their account with the pre-mapped role assignments**. No manual account creation, no permission-request tickets, no errors.

The `commons` project pattern from 1.2 pairs well with SSO/JIT — JIT-provisioned users get access to their functional area AND the shared assets project via SAML attribute mapping.

---

## 👑 **Workspace owner account**

The **workspace owner (root) account** is the most privileged account in your Workato workspace. Special safeguards apply:

> 📌 Four rules for the workspace owner account:
> 
> - **🚫 Do NOT use the workspace owner account for everyday tasks.** Create a **separate Workspace admin account** and use it for platform administration.
> - **🔒 Enable 2FA on the workspace owner account** — non-negotiable.
> - **🔐 Restrict access to workspace owner credentials** to authorized users/security team only.
> - **♻️ Rotate passwords after each use.**

> ⚠️ Treat the workspace owner account the way SREs treat root/emergency access accounts on production servers: **use it only for the specific tasks that require it, then re-lock it**. Never leave it in daily rotation.

### 🧠 Quick recall

- What authentication protocol does Workato use for SSO? (SAML-based Single Sign-On)
- What happens when SSO is **enforced** on a team? (All members must authenticate through the IdP — Workato username/password logins are disabled for the team.)
- What's the automated onboarding pattern that eliminates manual account creation? (`_____` provisioning) (SSO/JIT — Just-in-Time)
- Should you use the workspace owner account for daily platform admin? (No — create a separate Workspace admin account for daily use.)
- What must be enabled on the workspace owner account? (Two-factor authentication — 2FA)
- What should happen to the workspace owner password after each use? (It should be rotated.)
- Who should have access to workspace owner credentials? (Authorized users / security team only — nobody else.)
- If SSO isn't in use, what's the secondary auth mechanism? (Enforce 2FA.)

---

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **SSO first, 2FA fallback** — the two preferred authentication approaches.
- **SAML SSO** is Workato's SSO protocol; **enforce it** to fully disable username/password logins.
- **SSO/JIT provisioning** is the gold-standard onboarding flow — automatic account creation with pre-mapped roles on first login.
- **Workspace owner is like root** — separate everyday admin account, 2FA-locked, restricted credential access, password rotation after every use.
- **Session logout** should align with org-wide security policy — configure it, don't leave the default.
