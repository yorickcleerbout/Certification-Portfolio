## 🔐 **Principle of least privilege**

A fundamental aspect of team security is controlling **how different assets and business applications are accessed** to protect them from unauthorized changes.

> 📌 To enforce a robust governance model, apply the **principle of least privilege** when defining security policies.

Concretely: **grant minimum privileges** to every user and app connection account necessary for its function. This limits any damage from accidental modifications or errors.

> 💡 _Example 1:_ If you're only extracting a list of accounts from your CRM periodically, use a **service account with read-only access** — not a full-access account.
> 
> _Example 2:_ Team members working on HR automation **don't need access to finance automation projects**. Scope their access accordingly.

---

## 🎯 **Need for access control**

Three drivers behind access control in a Workato workspace:

- **🔌 Workato connects to many enterprise applications** and processes data across environments — the connection surface is wide.
- **🛡️ Company business systems must be secured** against unintended activities by platform users, and sensitive data must not be accessible to unauthorized groups.
- **⚖️ Reduce the risk of data security violations** by aligning platform access with the user's organizational role.

---

## 🎭 **Using Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**

> 📌 Workato's **RBAC capabilities** let you **group different asset permissions** for effective assignment and management of user access based on their job function.

Three principles:

- **Apply least privilege** — users get access only to the assets and platform capabilities required to do their work.
- **Group permissions by role** — much easier to manage than per-user permissions.
- **Follow a structured process** — the RBAC design is a three-step process, covered below.

---

### 📋 Step 1: Assess your organization & teams

Before designing roles, understand the org:

- **🏢 Identify teams** across the organization that require platform access to build automation, and list various project activities.
- **🤝 Determine how teams collaborate** across automation project lifecycles to establish areas of responsibility and ownership.
- **📊 Use a RACI matrix** (**Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed**) to document the automation operating model across teams — streamlines communication and governance.
- **📜 Consider specific organizational security and compliance needs.**

> 📌 A **RACI matrix** is your best documentation tool for cross-team automation operating models. It clarifies who _does_ the work vs. who _approves_ it vs. who _advises_ vs. who _just needs to know_.

---

### 🎯 Step 2: Determine roles & responsibilities

Design the roles themselves:

- **🎭 Determine common roles required across teams** and identify asset permissions based on those roles.
- **🎨 Use custom roles** to enforce least privilege.
- **🏷️ Namespace all roles by teams** — with **one exception: the workspace admin role**, which is org-wide.
- **♻️ Avoid role proliferation** — balance administrative overhead. **Clone similar roles between teams** and restrict access only to relevant projects.
- **🔒 Separate duties by environment** — e.g. **prevent developers from having edit access in production**.

> ⚠️ **Every role except the workspace admin role should be namespaced by teams.** This is a hard rule for large orgs — otherwise cross-team permission bleed becomes unmanageable.

---

### 🔄 Step 3: Review & adjust roles

Roll out gradually and iterate:

- **🧪 Start small** — apply RBAC with a small group/core team members first, validate the custom roles, modify as needed **before rolling out across teams**.
- **📝 Document an exception approval process** for granting additional access to team members on a need-basis.
- **🔍 Perform periodic maintenance** to avoid overlapping roles, remove excessive permissions, and adjust existing policies per business needs.

---

## 📊 **Example role definitions**

Four common role archetypes in a Workato workspace:

|Organization Role|Scope|Example Role|What this role does|
|---|---|---|---|
|**👑 Platform Admin**|Platform|Platform Admin|Oversight and ownership of the full Workato platform. Full access to the platform and manages core security configuration.|
|**🔄 Operations Analyst**|Functional Area (team projects only)|HR Operations|Allowed to **promote recipes between environments** — ensures control over changes moving into production. Often restricted to a specific functional area in larger organizations.|
|**🛠️ Developer**|Functional Area (team projects only)|HR Builder|Allowed to **create/edit recipes and connections in non-production** environments. **Does NOT have access** to create/edit recipes or connections in production.|
|**📊 Business Analyst**|Functional Area (team projects only)|HR Analyst|Allowed to **create/edit recipes in non-production only**. **No connection access**. **No production edit access**. Enables isolated, controlled development for team members who need only recipe-authoring rights.|

> 📌 The **scope pattern** matters: Platform Admin is org-wide; every other role is functional-area-scoped (namespaced by team). This is a concrete example of the "namespace by teams" rule from Step 2.

> 📌 **Notice the separation of duties**: the Developer role can build in non-prod but **cannot deploy to prod** — that's the Operations Analyst's job. This split prevents any single person from developing AND deploying a critical change.

- What foundational security principle drives access control? (Principle of least privilege — grant minimum privileges necessary for a function.)
- What acronym breakdown covers the RACI matrix roles? (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)
- Which role scope is org-wide? (Platform Admin — the only exception to "namespace by teams".)
- Which role can promote recipes between environments? (Operations Analyst)

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Least privilege** is the foundational rule — grant only the access necessary for the job.
- **RBAC design is a 3-step process**: assess org & teams → determine roles → review & adjust.
- Use a **RACI matrix** to document the cross-team automation operating model.
- **Namespace roles by teams** — the workspace admin role is the only exception.
- **Separate duties by environment** — Developers build in non-prod, Operations Analysts promote to prod. No single person does both.
- **Four common roles**: Platform Admin (org-wide), Operations Analyst (promotes), Developer (builds recipes + connections in non-prod), Business Analyst (recipes only in non-prod).
