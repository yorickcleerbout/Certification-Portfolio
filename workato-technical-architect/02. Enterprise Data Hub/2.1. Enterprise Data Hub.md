## 💎 **What is the Enterprise Data Hub Accelerator?**

**Core data** — customers, employees, orders, campaigns — is a critical driver of any modern digital business. It powers **analytics**, **process automation**, **decision rules**, **data science initiatives**, and more. Managing this data well matters a lot.

> 📌 **Workato's Enterprise Data Hub (EDH) Accelerator** provides a robust solution for **efficiently managing key master records** — ensuring a **single trusted source** that stays **cleansed, enriched, and always up-to-date** through **near real-time data synchronization**.

> 💡 EDH was introduced in the Technical Developer course (chapter 13.1) as one of the six flagship Automation Accelerators. This lesson goes deeper into when and why to use it.

---

## 🎯 **When is EDH the right fit?**

Four scenarios where EDH shines:

- **💰 You're exploring MDM solutions.** EDH is **not a full Master Data Management (MDM) solution** — but in many cases, that's exactly why it fits. You get most of what MDM offers **without the full MDM price tag**.
- **📤 Distributing master records to 5+ downstream applications.** Once the number of consumers of your master data crosses ~5, ad-hoc integrations become unmanageable and EDH's centralized-hub approach pays off.
- **🏆 Establishing a "golden record"** — master records are spread across multiple systems, but you want **a single source of truth** each downstream consumer agrees on.
- **🏗️ Building decoupled architecture** — makes it easy to **change source systems** or handle **large data acquisitions** (M&A, org restructures) **without modifying every downstream integration and automation**.

> 📌 The **decoupling benefit** is often the underrated one: EDH lets you swap out a source system (e.g. Workday → Successfactors) or ingest a newly-acquired company's data **without touching all your downstream recipes**. The hub absorbs the source change.

> ⚠️ **EDH is NOT a full MDM.** Full MDM platforms (Informatica MDM, Reltio, etc.) offer richer data stewardship, deep survivorship rules, and hierarchy management. EDH targets the **80% use case** at a much lower cost — same "lightweight, high-value" philosophy that defines Accelerators generally (chapter 13.1 in Technical Developer).

## ❓ **Supported databases**

> 📌 **Three databases are supported out of the box:**
> 
> - **❄️ Snowflake**
> - **🗄️ SQL Server**
> - **🐘 PostgreSQL**
> 
> EDH is **extendable** to others as needed.

The choice reflects EDH's positioning: **cloud data warehouse (Snowflake)**, **enterprise RDBMS (SQL Server)**, and **modern open-source RDBMS (PostgreSQL)** — cover the three most common master-data storage patterns in modern architectures.

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **EDH = Workato Enterprise Data Hub Accelerator** — the lightweight master-data solution for organizations that need MDM benefits without the MDM price tag.
- **Best-fit scenarios**: distributing master records to 5+ downstream apps; needing a golden record from data spread across multiple sources; building decoupled architecture to survive source-system changes and acquisitions.
- **Near real-time sync** keeps the master data cleansed, enriched, and current across consumers.
- **Three supported databases out of the box**: Snowflake, SQL Server, PostgreSQL — **extendable** to others.
- **Not a full MDM** — it targets the common 80% of MDM use cases at a much lower cost. When you need deep stewardship, survivorship rules, or hierarchy management, look at a proper MDM platform.
