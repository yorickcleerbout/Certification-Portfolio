## 🏗️ **Framework structure — domains and levers**

The GEARS Framework has a **two-level hierarchy**:

> 📌 **Domains → Levers**
> 
> **Domains** (five core capability areas) are further **divided into levers** — actionable building blocks that provide a blueprint for progression to the next maturity level.

Three foundational ideas:

- **🎯 Five core domains** represent organizational capabilities — including **employee skills, processes, and technology operations**.
- **📈 Progressive adoption** — apply relevant principles based on your business strategy and the current maturity of each domain. Don't try to boil the ocean.
- **🔧 Domains divided into levers** — an actionable blueprint for progression, and for greater alignment between the operating model and business objectives.

---

## ⚙️ **GEARS Framework levers**

> 📌 A **lever** is an area where you can make tangible improvements in your organization. Each improvement contributes to a **more effective, more efficient, and more manageable** automation practice.

Each domain of the framework can be broken down into these levers. The rest of the chapter walks through the levers of each domain — 3.4 (Govern), 3.5 (Enable), 3.6 (Adopt), 3.7 (Run), 3.8 (Scale).

---

### 💡 Two illustrative levers

Two examples that give a sense of how levers work:

- **📈 Business Technology Strategy** — this drives the **entire integration & automation practice** and therefore **affects every domain** in the framework. Not localized to one domain — it's foundational.
- **🔄 Recipe Lifecycle Management** — covers how your organization can **plan, implement, and operate automations** by applying Recipe Lifecycle Management processes (deeply covered in **Technical Developer chapter 8**).

> 💡 The Recipe Lifecycle Management topic connects back to what you studied in Technical Developer chapter 8 (Environment Management, Recipe Lifecycle Stages, Manifests & Packages, Release Management). GEARS treats RLCM as one of many levers within a broader operating model.

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Domains → levers** is the two-level structure of GEARS.
- **Five domains** cover employee skills, processes, and technology operations.
- **Levers are the actionable building blocks** — each one is an area for tangible improvement.
- **Progressive adoption** — apply relevant principles based on strategy and current maturity. Don't force every lever at every level all at once.
- Some levers, like **Business Technology Strategy**, cut across all domains — they're foundational rather than isolated to one area.
