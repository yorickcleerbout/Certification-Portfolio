## 🗓️ **The three-phase execution approach**

Adopting GEARS isn't a big-bang exercise — it happens in **three progressive phases**, each with distinct deliverables.

> 📌 The three phases map to increasing maturity levels:
> 
> - **🏗️ Phase 1: Foundation** — get the basics right
> - **🌱 Phase 2: Grow** — scale the practice
> - **🚀 Phase 3: Transform** — enterprise-wide adoption

---

## 🏗️ **Phase 1: Foundation**

The initial phase focuses on **building the foundation** for your integration & automation practice.

> 📌 **Foundation phase deliverables:**
> 
> - **🎯 First automation in production**
> - **📋 Automation operating model defined**

Everything else builds on these two — a working production automation and a documented model for how automation gets done.

---

## 🌱 **Phase 2: Grow**

The Grow phase focuses on **scaling** your integration and automation practice.

> 📌 **Grow phase deliverables:**
> 
> - **🍳 Multiple automations in production**
> - **🏢 Supporting more than one functional area**
> - **🛡️ Governance processes in operation**
> - **📖 Resource Center available**

Grow is where cross-functional scale kicks in — the practice serves multiple departments and has real governance running.

---

## 🚀 **Phase 3: Transform**

The Transform phase means a **multi-team automation practice has been established**.

> 📌 **Transform phase deliverables:**
> 
> - **👥 Workato community launched**
> - **🎓 Formalized training program in operation**
> - **📣 Internal adoption campaign started**
> - **🧩 Shared components available**

Transform is where the practice becomes **cultural** — training programs, active adoption campaigns, shared reusable components. It's no longer a project, it's a way the org works.

---

### 🧭 Phases mapped to the 3-step journey from 3.2

The three phases in this lesson map directly to the three-step journey from lesson 3.2:

|3.2 step|3.9 phase|Focus|
|---|---|---|
|Create a strong foundation|🏗️ Foundation|Security, QA, lifecycle|
|Build a culture of automation|🌱 Grow|Onboarding, enablement, adoption|
|Harness value at scale|🚀 Transform|Reporting, continuous improvement, wall-to-wall|

Same journey, different framings — the phases are the **milestones**, and the steps are the **activities** that move you between them.

## 📝 **Chapter recap — the seven key ideas**

The GEARS Framework in seven statements:

1. **🔠 GEARS is divided into five domains:**
    - **G**overn
    - **E**nable
    - **A**dopt
    - **R**un
    - **S**cale
2. **🎯 GEARS is a model** that can be adopted to **fully scale** enterprise automations.
3. **🛠️ GEARS helps resolve organizational issues** such as **decentralized teams and mismanaged allocations**.
4. **⚙️ Each domain is supported by levers** — each lever represents **key activities** and affects different organizational areas.
5. **🗓️ The phased approach has three maturity levels:**
    - Foundation
    - Grow
    - Transform
6. **📈 Higher maturity → easier to establish new levers** for each GEARS domain.
7. **🌐 Wall-to-wall automation** is achieved by **adopting GEARS as a planning model** for each domain's blueprint and its supporting levers.

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Three-phase execution**: **Foundation → Grow → Transform**, matched to increasing maturity.
- **Foundation** = first production automation + operating model defined.
- **Grow** = multiple automations, multiple functional areas, governance running, Resource Center live.
- **Transform** = community, training program, adoption campaigns, shared components — the practice becomes cultural.
- **Higher maturity = easier lever adoption** — each phase makes the next one easier.
- **Wall-to-wall automation** is the end goal — GEARS is the planning framework to get there.
