## ⚠️ **Common challenges of scaling automation**

Scaling an automation practice faces both **organizational and technical challenges**. Though some may seem generic, they ultimately represent **exactly what governance and automation solutions aim to solve**.

Four recurring challenges:

---

### 📥 Automation opportunities & delivery backlog

**Opportunities for automation grow every day** with newer technologies — and so do the **backlogs** for delivery teams. The gap between "what we could automate" and "what we've actually delivered" keeps widening.

---

### 🎓 Availability of specialized resources

**Specialized integration resources** (experienced integration engineers, legacy platform experts) are becoming **more difficult to find**. Scaling up on **legacy integration tools** becomes especially hard.

---

### 🔄 Automations need to change with the business

Business-aligned automations must change at the **pace of the business and market demands**.

> 📌 **Mental model shift**: adopt a **"build-to-adapt"** mentality — not the traditional **"build-to-last"** approach. Automations should be designed for change, not for permanence.

---

### ⚖️ Democratization with governance

**Democratizing access** to a broader range of personas requires **strong AND agile governance guardrails** — empowering teams **without compromising security**. Neither extreme works: too much lockdown blocks builders, too little exposes the organization.

---

## 🚀 **How GEARS helps — three steps to building an automation practice**

The GEARS Framework provides a way to navigate scaling challenges. It defines a **three-step journey**:

---

### 🏗️ Step 1: Create a strong foundation

> 📌 Establish **security policies, quality assurance, and automation lifecycle processes** so you can build and operate automation projects **without disruption**.

This is the "no chaos" phase — before you scale up, get the fundamentals right so the practice doesn't collapse under its own growth.

---

### 🎓 Step 2: Build a culture of automation

> 📌 Create a **well-defined enablement approach** to help people with different skills build automations. Support them through **onboarding**, and **foster adoption** by getting more people building — unlocking project delivery capacity.

The "many hands make light work" phase — spread the ability to build across the organization.

---

### 📊 Step 3: Harness value at scale

> 📌 Scale by ensuring each initiative aligns with **enterprise strategy** and pushes **business goals forward**. **Report business impact** and **continuously improve** the different business processes to become more efficient.

The "prove it's paying off" phase — measurable, reported, iterated.

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Four scaling challenges**: growing backlogs, scarce specialized resources, automations needing to keep pace with business change, and democratization vs. governance.
- **Build-to-adapt, not build-to-last** — the mindset that fits modern automation.
- **Three-step journey**: **Foundation** (security, QA, lifecycle) → **Culture** (enablement, onboarding, adoption) → **Value at scale** (strategic alignment, business impact reporting).
- Each step aligns with phases in 3.9's phased execution approach — **Foundation, Grow, Transform**.
