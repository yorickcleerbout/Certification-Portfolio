## ⚙️ **The three OPA features**

The On-Prem Agent has three key features that make it suitable for enterprise security: **Secure Tunnel**, **Complete Control**, and **Extensibility**.

---

### 🔒 Secure tunnel

> 📌 The OPA runs **inside the customer's datacenter** and establishes a **TLS 1.2 websocket tunnel** with Workato.

The direction of the connection matters:

> ⚠️ **OPA runs behind the firewall and connects OUT to Workato** — not the other way around. Workato never initiates a connection inbound to your network. This is what allows selective access to on-prem apps **without opening any ports in the corporate firewall**.

The net effect: significantly reduced attack surface, which is the part your InfoSec team will care about most.

---

### 🎛️ Complete control

The customer has **full and complete control** over what the agent can do. Authorization is configured per-app and per-recipe — Workato never gets blanket access to your on-prem environment.

---

### 🧩 Extensible

The OPA is **extensible** — you can build custom connectors that extend the agent's functionality to reach on-prem applications that **don't use HTTP APIs**. So if you have a legacy system that speaks a non-HTTP protocol, the OPA can still bridge it.

---

### 🧠 Quick recall

- Name the three OPA features. (Secure tunnel, complete control, extensibility)
- What protocol does the secure tunnel use? (`_____`) (TLS 1.2 websocket)
- Which direction does the OPA connect — IN from Workato, or OUT to Workato? (OUT — it runs behind the firewall and initiates the outbound connection.)
- Does using OPA require opening firewall ports? (No — that's the entire point of the outbound tunnel.)
- What kind of apps can extensibility bring under OPA's reach? (Apps that don't use HTTP APIs — legacy systems with non-HTTP protocols.)

---

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Secure tunnel** — TLS 1.2 websocket from inside the datacenter, **outbound** to Workato, **no firewall ports opened**.
- **Complete control** — customer authorizes the agent's behavior; access is granular per-app and per-recipe.
- **Extensible** — custom connectors can reach on-prem apps that don't speak HTTP.
