## ⚡ **Triggers for recipe and job monitoring**

Using **RecipeOps by Workato as a trigger event**, you can build recipes that **manage other recipes**. The connector exposes triggers across three categories: recipe/job monitoring, connection monitoring, and additional system-level monitoring.

---

### 🛑 Recipe Started/Stopped by Workato/User

Build contingencies for key recipes that **should or should not be started/stopped**. Common patterns:

- **🔁 Enforce restart logic** when a recipe is stopped unexpectedly.
- **📢 Escalation policies** to notify key personnel when a recipe stops.

> 📌 Workato stops a recipe when it encounters **non-recoverable errors** — i.e. when Workato has attempted the trigger or action repeatedly without success.

> 📌 **Specific threshold:** Workato will stop an active recipe after **60 cumulative authentication errors** in the trigger.

Monitor scope: **all recipes** OR **selected recipes** (provide their Recipe IDs).

---

### ❌ Job Failed

The **Job Failed** trigger monitors all active recipes (or selected recipes) for **failed jobs in real time**. Useful for ensuring key recipes are monitored with automated corrective actions and notifications.

To monitor specific recipes only, provide their Recipe IDs.

---

## 🔌 **Triggers for connection monitoring**

Workato connects to many apps to build recipes. RecipeOps lets you **monitor API call usage** and **app account status** to keep those connections healthy.

---

### 🌐 API Monitoring

When implementing APIs through Workato's API Platform, **exceeding recipe limitations** can cause unexpected errors. To prevent service disruptions, Workato provides **built-in policies and monitoring** to manage API consumption effectively.

RecipeOps triggers exist for managing and monitoring the health of your API recipes.

---

### 🪪 Account Monitoring

Monitor an app connection by verifying its account state:

- **🔌 Connection status**
- **🛡️ Permission status**
- **🔑 API access credentials**
- **👥 Invited members**

Use these trigger steps to ensure connectors are healthy — a **fundamental prerequisite** for successful recipes.

---

## 📊 **Triggers for additional monitoring**

### 📈 Usage threshold reached

> 📌 The **Usage Threshold Reached** trigger fires when a user hits **task usage thresholds** for their plan. Users can receive an **event for every 10% of usage**.

Common use case: monitor workspace task consumption proactively against the plan ceiling.

> ⚠️ **Before you can use this trigger, it must be enabled in your account.** Contact your Workato Success Representative.

---

### 🧠 Quick recall

- Workato stops a recipe after how many cumulative authentication errors in the trigger? (`_____`) (60)
- The Usage Threshold Reached trigger fires at every `_____` of usage. (10%)
- Does the Usage Threshold trigger work by default? (No — it must be enabled by your Workato Success Representative.)
- What kind of errors cause Workato to stop a recipe? (Non-recoverable errors — repeated unsuccessful trigger/action attempts.)
- Name three categories of RecipeOps triggers. (Recipe/job monitoring; connection monitoring; additional system monitoring)
- The Job Failed trigger can monitor what scope? (All active recipes OR selected recipes by Recipe ID.)

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Three trigger categories**: recipe/job monitoring (Recipe Started/Stopped, Job Failed), connection monitoring (API, Account), and additional (Usage Threshold).
- **60 cumulative auth errors** in the trigger = automatic recipe stop. Worth knowing the exact number.
- **Usage Threshold** fires every 10% — but **must be enabled** by your Workato Success Representative first.
- Most monitoring triggers can target **all recipes** or **selected recipes** (by Recipe ID).
