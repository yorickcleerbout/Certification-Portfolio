## 🎯 **What can RecipeOps by Workato do for you?**

RecipeOps enables **automated monitoring, management, and recovery** of active recipes. Five common use cases:

- **🛑 Stop a recipe that has too many errors.**
- **▶️ Start a recipe that was stopped** due to scheduled downtime.
- **📊 Get logs from multiple recipes** simultaneously.
- **🐛 Pick out non-graceful error log entries** from multiple recipes.
- **🔁 Rerun failed jobs.**

---

## ⚖️ **Error Monitoring Action vs RecipeOps**

A natural question: why not just use the **Error Monitoring and Handling Action** during recipe building? Both handle errors — but they're optimized for different scenarios.

| |**🛡️ Error Monitoring Action**|**🤖 RecipeOps by Workato**|
|---|---|---|
|**Scope**|Added during recipe building; **specific to ONE recipe**|Workspace-level; **reusable across MULTIPLE recipes**|
|**Workflow integration**|Must be **built into your workflow logic**|Lives **outside the recipe** — no workflow integration needed|
|**Granular control**|✅ Allows granular control over errors|✅ Also offers granular control as part of a custom workflow bot|
|**Recipe behavior**|Recipes **continue to run** unless workflow includes a Stop Job task|Can **stop / restart / start recipes** based on variables|
|**Best for**|**Specific errors** or a **small number of recipes**|Monitoring and automating a **large number of recipes**|

> 📌 **The decision rule:** if you're handling a specific error inside one recipe → Error Monitoring Action. If you're managing many recipes at workspace scale → RecipeOps.

---

## ✅ **Benefits**

By leveraging RecipeOps, you establish **intelligent operations** that enhance reliability, efficiency, and scalability of automated workflows:

- **📊 Awareness & prevention** with reports.
- **🚑 Quick recovery** of critical systems through error message customization and notifications.
- **🔁 Automatic rerun** of recipes — no manual recovery steps needed.
- **📢 Notifications upon failures** — increased awareness.
- **🛡️ Mitigated impact** on mission-critical automations via automatic restart.

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **RecipeOps shines at workspace scale** — managing many recipes from outside their own workflow logic.
- **Error Monitoring Action shines at recipe scope** — handling specific errors inside one recipe's workflow.
- **Both offer granular control** — choosing between them is about **scope** (one vs many) and **placement** (inside vs outside the recipe).
- **Five canonical use cases**: stop too-errored recipes, start scheduled-downtime recipes, multi-recipe log retrieval, surface non-graceful errors, rerun failed jobs.
