## 🚀 **What are Automation Accelerators?**

Workato already offers **community recipes** and **recipe collections** to help you on your automation journey. But what if you need a more **turn-key solution** with a more direct route?

> 📌 **Automation Accelerators** are **holistic, end-to-end implementations** that get you to your automation destination quicker. They are **asset packages** that work with your data and applications to solve common automation use cases across specific environments.

The origin story: over the years, Workato has worked with thousands of customers across many industries and noticed that many customers have **similar workflows** and some recipes have **functionality with high reusability**. Accelerators are Workato's response — **collections of pre-packaged Workato assets** designed to work together for common use cases across industries.

> 📌 **Accelerators are delivered as a ZIP file** and can be quickly and easily imported into your Workato environment. (Same pattern as manifests/packages in chapter 8.3.)

The benefit: reduce time and money spent on research & development, and focus more on what actually matters.

---

## 📦 **Package contents**

> 📌 Every Accelerator package can contain any combination of **five asset types**:

- **🍳 Pre-built recipes** — ready to run, minimal configuration.
- **⚙️ Custom recipes** — tailored to the accelerator's use case.
- **🧩 Solution components** — additional supporting assets.
- **📊 Reference data** — lookup tables, seed data.
- **📖 Instructional guides** — how-to documentation.

These bundle everything you need to get running — no piece-by-piece downloads.

## ⭐ **Benefits of Automation Accelerators**

Accelerators let you take a typical automation solution and apply **personalized logic to a package with high reusability**. Concretely, they enable you to:

- **🤝 Leverage your solution community.** Best practices are baked in from day one — Workato has collected ideas from thousands of implementations.
- **⚡ Deliver outcomes faster.** With a jump start for **~80% of the solution in one ZIP file**, you spend less time figuring things out and more time improving.

> 📌 The **80% jump start** is the headline number worth remembering — Accelerators aren't finished solutions, they're substantial foundations you customize.

## 🏷️ **Accelerator asset naming conventions**

Accelerators apply the **Recipe Lifecycle Management (RLCM) naming best practices** covered in **chapter 8.1** — with a specific prefix scheme so you can identify Accelerator assets inside your workspace.

> 💡 _Quick RLCM recap from chapter 8.1:_ naming conventions promote consistency and provide a common vocabulary between teams. E.g. `HRD 123-01 Create G-Suite Account` tells you the HR team manages recipe 01 of Automation #123. Accelerators follow the same principle, plus a **prefix**.

---

### 🔤 The four Accelerator prefixes

> 📌 All Accelerator assets are prefixed with one of four codes:

|Prefix|Full name|Purpose|
|---|---|---|
|**`Sub`**|Subscriber|**Decouples your functional recipes from Accelerator assets** — the layer between your workspace and Accelerator internals.|
|**`REC`**|Recipe|The **core recipes** of the Accelerator.|
|**`CALL`**|Callable Recipes|Recipes that can be **called from within other recipes** (reusable business logic).|
|**`CON`**|Connections|Connections used for the **internal functioning of Accelerators** — e.g. collecting logs, sending notifications.|

The prefix scheme lets you scan your workspace and immediately identify what's Accelerator-owned vs. what's your own custom work.
