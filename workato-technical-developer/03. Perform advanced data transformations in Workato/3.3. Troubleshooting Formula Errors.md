## 🚨 **Design-time errors vs runtime errors**

> 📌 Workato recipes can fail in two distinct phases: **design-time errors** (before the recipe runs) and **runtime errors** (during execution). Understanding which one you're looking at is the first step in troubleshooting.

---

### 🏗️ Design-time errors

Design-time errors happen during the **development phase** — _before_ you click **Start recipe**.

Common causes:

- **🔌 Unestablished connectivity** — invalid credentials, missing permissions, or incomplete configuration prevent a valid connection with external services or apps.
- **📭 Missing input fields** — required input fields in triggers or actions that have no value or datapill.
- **❓ Unavailable fields** — data fields used in the recipe no longer exist in the app (deletion, renaming).
- **🐛 Invalid datapills / formula errors** — incorrect formulas, syntax errors, misspellings, or logical bugs.

> ⚠️ Design-time errors **prevent the recipe from starting or being tested** — they must be resolved first.

**Where to find them:** error notifications appear at the **top of the recipe editor**. You can click highlighted step numbers to jump directly to the problem step.

---

### ▶️ Runtime errors

Runtime errors occur during **execution** — within jobs, _after_ the recipe has been started.

Common causes:

- **⚙️ Action errors** — problems during action execution: data formatting errors, API call failures, processing errors.
- **⚡ Trigger errors** — failures executing a trigger, often due to misconfigurations or external systems returning unexpected data.
- **🔧 Internal errors** — issues within the Workato platform: SQL collections, parsers, readers, lookup tables.
- **🌐 Upstream/downstream application errors** — failures originating in external systems your recipe interacts with.

> ⚠️ Runtime errors **disrupt successful completion** of a recipe and may affect the current job _or_ subsequent jobs.

**Where to find them:** monitor **job history** in your Workato account, review error messages and logs, and identify whether the source is an action, a trigger, or an external system.

---

### 🧠 Quick recall

- Design-time errors happen `_____` the recipe starts; runtime errors happen `_____` the recipe starts. (Before; during/after)
- A recipe references a Salesforce field that was renamed last week. Design-time or runtime error? (Design-time — unavailable field)
- A recipe is running and a downstream API returns a 500. Design-time or runtime? (Runtime — upstream/downstream application error)
- Where are design-time errors surfaced? (At the top of the recipe editor — click highlighted step numbers to navigate.)
- Where do you investigate runtime errors? (Job history — review error messages and logs to identify whether it's an action, trigger, or external system.)
- Name two design-time error causes and two runtime error causes. (Design-time: any two of connectivity, missing input fields, unavailable fields, invalid datapills. Runtime: any two of action errors, trigger errors, internal errors, upstream/downstream app errors.)

---

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Design-time errors** = before the recipe runs. Causes: connectivity, missing input fields, unavailable fields, invalid datapills. **Surfaced in the editor**; recipe can't start until resolved.
- **Runtime errors** = during execution. Causes: action errors, trigger errors, internal errors, upstream/downstream app errors. **Surfaced in job history**; disrupt completion and may cascade to later jobs.
- The first troubleshooting question is always _"editor or job history?"_ — that one decision splits your debugging path.
