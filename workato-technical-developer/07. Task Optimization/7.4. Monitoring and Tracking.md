## 📊 **Monitoring task consumption**

You've identified, assessed, and optimized your high-consumption recipes — but ongoing visibility matters too. Workato provides monitoring at **three levels**, plus options for automated reporting.

> 📌 Task consumption can be tracked at the **job**, **recipe**, or **account** level. Beyond the UI, you can include task metrics in regular administrative reports and automate monitoring with **[RecipeOps](https://docs.workato.com/recipes/tasks-counters.html#task-usage-management-by-recipe)**

---

### 🧩 Monitoring at the job level

Open any individual job to see the total tasks used.

> ⚠️ A recipe containing calls to other **recipe functions** will **not** show the total tasks across all child jobs at the parent level — each child job has its own task usage details that must be reviewed separately.

---

### 🍳 Monitoring at the recipe level

Recipe-level task usage lives under the **Settings tab** of the recipe. You can also filter specific recipes and time periods from the **Dashboard**.

---

### 🏢 Monitoring at the account level

Monthly **account usage** is available in the **Subscription tab** of Account Settings. It shows monthly and overall task consumption against your subscription plan.

---

### 🧠 Quick recall

- Name the three monitoring levels. (Job, recipe, account)
- Where do you find recipe-level task usage? (Settings tab of the recipe — or filter from the Dashboard.)
- Where do you find account-level usage? (Subscription tab in Account Settings)
- A recipe calls child recipe functions. Does the parent job's task count include child task counts? (No — each child has its own task usage details.)
- What tool automates task monitoring and management beyond the UI? (RecipeOps)

## 🧱 **Task fundamentals — from the knowledge check**

The knowledge check below tests several **task accounting fundamentals** that aren't fully explained in this lesson body. Worth surfacing here for study:

> 📌 **What is a task?** A **unit of work that occurs every time a recipe performs an action**. (Not the action itself, not a child job, not control flow logic — the _execution_ of an action.)

> 📌 **What does NOT count as a task:**
> 
> - **Triggers** — the event arrival itself doesn't consume a task.
> - **Control flow logic** — `if/else`, `repeat`, `try/catch` skeletons don't count.
> - **Error handling logic** — the error block structure itself isn't a task.

> 📌 **Conditional behavior:** an action inside a control flow construct (IF branch, repeat loop) **only counts toward task consumption if the controlling condition evaluates to true** and the action actually executes.

> 📌 **Failed actions:** **a failed action is NOT counted as a task.** (Successful steps before the failure still count — see Foundations Level 1, module 4 for context.)

These rules explain why optimization strategies focus on what they do: collapsing many actions into batch actions, replacing repeat loops with single formula-mode steps, using trigger conditions to eliminate jobs entirely, etc.

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Three monitoring levels**: job (individual run, no rollup for child recipe functions), recipe (Settings tab or Dashboard filter), account (Subscription tab).
- **RecipeOps** is the tool for automated task monitoring and management beyond the UI.
- **Task accounting rules** (mostly from the quiz): a task = a unit of work when an action runs; triggers, control flow, and error handling don't count; conditional actions count only when actually executed; failed actions don't count.
