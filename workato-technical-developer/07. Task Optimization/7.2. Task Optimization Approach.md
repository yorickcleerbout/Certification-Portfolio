## 🎯 **The three-step optimization approach**

Once you understand _how_ tasks get consumed (chapter 7.1), the next step is identifying _which_ recipes to target.

> 📌 The task optimization approach is a **three-step process**:
> 
> 1. **🔍 Identify** — set up a process to find high-consumption recipes.
> 2. **📋 Assess** — analyze candidates for business needs and available options.
> 3. **⚡ Optimize** — apply strategies to improve performance.

The rest of this lesson covers steps 1 and 2; step 3 (the strategies themselves) is covered in 7.3.

---

## 🔍 **Step 1: Identify high-consumption recipes**

Make recipe identification an **automated, proactive process** in your workspace — not something you do reactively after problems appear. Flag high-consumption recipes as candidates for optimization, then apply strategies to bring task counts down to acceptable levels.

Two approaches, used in different situations:

---

### 🔮 The predictive approach

> 📌 Use the **predictive approach** when you **don't have much historical data** — typically when you've just put new automations into production.

Within one or two months of starting a recipe, Workato extrapolates from the past month's usage to **predict annual task usage**. You can use that prediction to decide whether to optimize the recipe without waiting months for trends to emerge.

---

### 📜 The reactive approach

Use the **reactive approach** once you have significant runtime history. Three steps:

1. **📈 Sort automations by lifetime task consumption** to surface candidate recipes.
2. **🗓️ Use fixed, considerable periods** — 3 to 12 months — for comparing usage across recipes.
3. **📊 Watch for upward trends** — periodic spikes or gradual growth in task usage.

---

- Name the three steps of the optimization approach. (Identify, Assess, Optimize)
- When does the predictive approach apply? (When you don't have much historical data — typically just after putting new automations into production.)

- Reactive approach — what do you sort by? (Lifetime task consumption metric)

## 📋 **Step 2: Assess needs**

Once you've identified candidates, **assess them by business criticality** before picking optimization strategies.

> 📌 At a high level, every recipe is either **real-time** or **batch/deferred**. Ask whether any near-real-time or real-time recipes can be converted to deferred/batched processing without impacting productivity.

> 💡 _Example:_ a recipe with a 5-minute polling interval may be convertible to an hourly or daily sync without anyone noticing — but the task savings are real.

---

### 🛠️ Optimization strategy categories

After assessment, you apply optimization strategies that fall into **three categories**:

|Category|What it controls|
|---|---|
|**🎯 Trigger strategies**|How automations are triggered — controls the **number of jobs** for a given recipe.|
|**📦 Batch strategies**|How multiple events are processed together — optimizes **multiple events per job**.|
|**⚡ Recipe efficiency strategies**|How business logic is implemented — uses alternative approaches to run logic more efficiently per step.|

Notice the alignment with the three key factors from 7.1:

- **Trigger strategies** ↔ factor 1 (number of jobs)
- **Batch strategies** ↔ factor 2 (events per job)
- **Efficiency strategies** ↔ factor 3 (efficient processing)

---

- Each strategy category corresponds to one of the key factors of task usage — match them. (Trigger ↔ number of jobs; batch ↔ events per job; efficiency ↔ efficient processing)
- A recipe with a 5-minute polling interval could potentially be converted to what? (An hourly or daily sync — if the use case isn't time-critical.)
- Real-time vs batch/deferred — what should you ask during assessment? (Whether any near-real-time recipes can be safely converted to deferred processing.)

---

## 🚀 **Module key takeaways**

- **Three-step process**: Identify → Assess → Optimize.
- **Identify** with either predictive (low history, ~1–2 months out) or reactive (significant history, sort by lifetime task consumption).
- **Assess** by business criticality — can real-time become batch/deferred without impact?
- **Strategy categories align with the 3 key factors** from 7.1: Trigger ↔ jobs, Batch ↔ events per job, Efficiency ↔ processing logic.
